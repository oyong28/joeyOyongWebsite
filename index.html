Hi, I’m Joey Oyong. I am a developer, designer, and support specialist who brings together problem solving, creativity, and reliable systems thinking. My background spans production support for Navy Federal, full stack web development, and hands-on creative work across branding, posters, and client websites.

I started my career in technical support with Accenture on the Navy Federal DEPS team, where I handled live incidents, root cause analysis, ServiceNow workflows, and troubleshooting across pipelines, APIs, integrations, and SQL layers. I learned how to stay calm in high pressure situations and communicate clearly when teams depend on fast and accurate information.

As a developer, I build with React, JavaScript, Python, Flask, HTML, and CSS. I deploy all my work on a self-hosted Raspberry Pi stack using NGINX, HTTPS, Certbot, reverse proxy routing, systemd services, Git, SQLite, and WireGuard. I enjoy working end to end, from writing code to managing the infrastructure that runs it.

Alongside my engineering work, I design posters, event graphics, brand assets, and custom UI elements for clients. I build and maintain websites on Squarespace and cPanel, working with custom CSS, code injection, DNS configuration, store setup, and hands-on troubleshooting. I create visuals using GIMP, Canva, and Sketchbook, which allows me to produce clean, consistent designs for both web and print projects.

I also bring years of hands-on problem solving from my time working as a journeyman electrician, which shaped how I approach systems, safety, and real world troubleshooting.

I enjoy projects that combine technical and creative thinking. I am a strong fit for roles where development, application support, and design intersect, and I bring a builder’s mindset to every team I work with. When I’m not coding or designing, I enjoy climbing, hiking, fishing, and painting.

What this is

This is a straightforward website system for artists who need one place to send fans, venues, and collaborators. It keeps the essentials visible and removes the busywork that makes most sites feel hard to maintain.

It is not a custom web build. It is a proven layout and setup process that stays within clear boundaries.

What you get
  • Clean navigation built for fans and venues.
  • A layout that works on mobile and desktop.
  • Social and streaming links placed where people actually look.
  • A simple contact path for booking and collaboration.
  • Basic SEO and social preview setup so links share well.
  • A content checklist so you know what to provide.
  • A handoff that explains what you can update yourself.
  • A system that stays calm and consistent as your career grows.
After it’s live
  • You update shows, links, and media yourself.
  • The structure stays consistent so you are not reworking it later.
  • You use a simple handoff guide that shows what to update and what to leave alone.
